A Metamodel Proposal

As a first step towards a Smart Assessment Framework, we devised a metamodel with the elements of a Smart Assessment and their relationships. The work was presented in the 14th OTM/IFAC/IFIP International Workshop on Enterprise Integration, Interoperability and Networking (EI2N 2019).

Characterising Smart Systems

The idea of the Smart Assessment is based on the use systems with smart capabilities to support assessments in enterprises. Therefore, understanding what makes a system smart is relevant to guide the development of such systems. Our work, published in the Computers in Industry journal, presented a Systematic Literature Review followed by a Text Mining approach to analyse definitions of smart systems in the scientific literature with the objective to provide a description of the most common characteristics of smart systems.

  • Read the preprint

The Smart Assessment Framework

A conceptual framework devised to guide the development of smarter assessment approaches based on the integration of capabilities from smart systems to carry out the assessment process has been proposed in the 21st IFAC World Congress in 2020. The framework was defined through a conceptual model described using the ArchiMate modelling language. The model links Motivation, Business and Application elements that are present in the Smart Assessment scope.

Hervé Panetto

My name is Hervé Panetto. I am a Professor at University of Lorraine.
I am Director of international relations at TELECOM Nancy, School of Engineering in Information Technology, associate school of Institut Mines-Telecom. I am teaching Enterprise Information Systems. I conducts research at CRAN (Research Centre for Automatic Control), Joint Research Unit (UMR 7039) associated with University of Lorraine and CNRS, and member of the Research Federation Charles Hermite. Béatrix Barafort

Béatrix Barafort graduated as a Software Engineer in the Conservatoire National des Arts et Métiers (France) and has worked from 1988 to 1996 in a software house in Lyon for software development projects in banks and insurance companies. Then she joined the Luxembourg Institute of Science and Technology (LIST) where she has led R&D process assessment and improvement projects based on the ISO/IEC 15504/330xx standard (Process Assessment), mostly in IT Service Management, and in non-IT applications. She is actively involved in standardization activities in ISO/IEC JTC1 SC7 (Software and Systems Engineering with Process Assessment as a particular interest) and in ISO/IEC JTC1 SC40 (IT Service Management and IT Governance). She was editor of the published ISO/IEC 20000-4:2010 standard for an IT Service Management Process Reference Model.
